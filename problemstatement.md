**Problem Statement**

In many educational institutions, evaluating student performance and identifying students who may struggle academically is a challenging task. Teachers often rely on traditional assessment methods such as periodic tests, assignments, and attendance records, but it becomes difficult to analyze all these factors together to predict a student’s final performance in examinations.

Students’ academic results are influenced by several factors including study hours, attendance percentage, previous test scores, assignment completion, and participation in academic activities. However, predicting the final outcome based on these multiple variables manually can be inefficient and may not always provide accurate insights.

The main problem addressed in this project is the lack of an automated system that can analyze student data and provide early predictions about their academic results. Without such predictive insights, educational institutions may not be able to identify students who need additional academic support before the final examination results are announced.

This project aims to develop a machine learning-based Student Result Predictor that can analyze historical student data and learn patterns affecting academic outcomes. By using data analysis and predictive modeling techniques, the system can estimate whether a student is likely to pass or fail based on input parameters.

The predictive system can assist teachers and institutions in making data-driven decisions, enabling them to identify at-risk students early and implement corrective measures such as extra guidance, tutoring, or academic counseling. Ultimately, the goal of this project is to improve student success rates and support better academic planning through the application of machine learning techniques.
